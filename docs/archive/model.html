Model win probability vs market price, general elections only, in states whose primaries (incl. runoffs) are decided. Bars show edge = model Dem win prob − market Dem prob (market vig-normalized D/(D+R)). Blue right = the model likes the Democrat more than the market; red left = the model likes the Republican more. ⚠ SPLIT = the win-probability model and the separate margin model pick different winners — treat those as toss-ups with no edge (they're drawn hollow in the chart and dimmed in the table). Yellow poll counts (<5) = thin data; big edge + thin polls usually means the market knows something the polls don't.
